I made this infographic using Canva because it was the easiest option for me. I am not very experienced with designing, so Canva helped a lot with ready templates and simple tools. It took me some time to adjust things like spacing and colors, but overall it was manageable and kind of interesting to work on.

In my infographic, I included five topics: password security awareness, internet usage awareness, email etiquette awareness, social media awareness, and cybercrime awareness. I didn’t go into too much detail, instead I tried to keep the points short and easy to understand. I focused on things that we usually ignore, like reusing passwords or clicking random links without thinking.

While doing this task, I realized that we use the internet daily but don’t always follow safe practices. This made me more aware of small habits that can actually make a big difference. I also learned how to present information in a simple and clear format instead of writing long explanations. Overall, it was a useful experience and not as boring as I expected.
